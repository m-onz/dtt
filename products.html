State of the art drone detection

We made the decision to avoid using radio / RF for drone detection because we believe it has some major flaws and can be easily circumvented. The cost of RF, thermal and some radar/lidar systems is also a barrier to those seeking an affordable detection system.

To bypass an RF drone detection system worth millions of pounds and employed by a major airport involves buying a DIY drone kit... and modifying it to use any line of sight communication method.

Drone detection with machine learning

We decided to take the route of using cameras and microphones to detect drones. This came with its own challenges but as we are a small team we can produce our system at a drastically reduced cost and pass on the savings to our customers.

Audio detection

The common argument against audio detection is that it is easily tricked by urban background noises and won't work beyond 50 meters. These are exactly the kind of challenges we where faced with but using machine learning its possible to get amazing results. We are happy to say that these are no longer issues for us as we can use audio detection as a robust companion to video detection.

Video detection

We also successfully track drones, day or night and at distance using cameras. Needless to say our system is never fooled by birds or other flying objects. We use infra-red cameras to save costs and avoid using thermal cameras completely.

We also have the ability to run our video drone detection on your existing video feeds. If you have invested a lot in your camera and surveillance infrastructure you can make that investment go further. This is one of the major reasons we decided to focus on making our drone detection system work effectively on commodity hardware.
